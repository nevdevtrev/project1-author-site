Neville Sexton was born in 1976 and grew up in Bray, Co. Wicklow, Ireland. His first published work was as a contributing short story author alongside Maeve Binchy, Cathy Kelly and other noted authors in the charity Christmas book, There’s No Place like Home at Christmas (2010, Mercier Press). His first book, Craig The Boy Who Lives (2011, Gill & Macmillan), emotionally and poignantly reflects upon his son’s short beautiful life, his tragic death and the author’s belief in his son’s contact from beyond. Stemming from this book a featured TV documentary, Love Never Dies, was shown and viewed by millions on ABC Australia and RTE Ireland. Craig The Boy Who Lives was also presented to the Irish President Mary McAleese, and the well-known Hollywood actor Gabriel Byrne wrote of the book – ‘This is a beautiful, unforgettably moving story about the true meaning of Life and Death… and what makes sense of both - Love.’

Alongside his non-fiction work Neville is also a keen fiction writer, focussing on YA Science Fiction & Fantasy. His first such book, "The Outsiders", is due for release January 2020.
